Exams

Digital exams


  • Exams take place according to the examination plans posted on Studynet by the Study Administration no later than December 1 (winter exams) and May 1 (summer exams).
  • You need to show up in good time, so you can be ready and have all your IT equipment in place at least 15 minutes before the exam begins.
  • Remember to bring your VIA ID Card or similar ID with your photo on.

    • Handing in on Paper

  • Remember to write your name, student number, date, page numbers and course on the cover sheet including all sheets of the paper.
  • You may only use a blue or a black pen. You are not allowed to use red writing on your exam paper, as red is for corrections made by the examiner. You are not allowed to use pencil.

    • You have max. three attempts for each exam.
    • You are automatically registered for exams and reexams.
    • If you do not show for an exam or a reexam, you will use one exam attempt.
    • If you fall sick on the day of an exam, you must submit a medical ertificate within four days to the study administration to not use an exam attempt.
    • You will be assessed on an individual basis, also when the exam is based on a group project.
    • Plagiarism and cheating on an exam is taken very seriously.
    • Read the criteria for test and rules for exams carefully!

    Digital exams are for project hand ins and other kind of hand ins and written examinations of all kind. Oral examinations do not use WISEflow.

    What is WISEflow?

    WISEflow is a digital platform for handling the process of examination - from the assignment is given to assessment has been made. It is a tool for students, teachers and the Studyadministration.
    WISEflow is being implementet to VIAs educational programmes gradually but is already widely used in VIA for both written and oral examinations.
